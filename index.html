Summary

I'm a Ph.D student at Michigan State University (MSU) under supervision of Dr. Yiying Tong. Before joining MSU, I received my Bachelor's Degree from Zhejiang University. (CV )

My primary research interests are geometric processing and physically based simulation. I'm also working on geometry based computitional biology. My series of papers regarding computational de-Rham Hodge theory and its application on analyzing biomolecules contribute significantly to the NSF grant entitled "De Rham-Hodge theory modeling and learning of biomolecular data", which awards more than $1.18M. (News)

I'm actively looking for research or engineering oriented jobs that involves geometry processing on various types of 3D geometric data common in graphics and vision tasks.
